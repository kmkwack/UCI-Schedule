The product idea

01

Start with the timetable.

ClassMate pulls real course and section data from each supported school, so students can tap the classes they are taking instead of manually rebuilding a calendar from scratch.

02

Add course judgment.

Course reviews sit directly on top of the schedule, helping students answer not just whether a class fits, but whether it is worth taking.

03

Keep discussion verified.

Students are verified as members of their school, but can post personally anonymously. That creates room for honest conversation without losing campus trust.

04

Scale only when the campus is ready.

We now support five universities and expand campus by campus where course data, student verification, and campus density can make the product reliable.

Company motto

Make college less scattered.

College is not one app. It is a schedule, a course search, a professor review, a room number, an anonymous post, a group message, a sports event, and a reminder you almost forgot. the seans exists to make those pieces feel like they belong together.

Why we started

The timetable is the most honest map of student life.

Every term, students rebuild the same context from scratch. They add classes, hunt for course reviews, ask who else is enrolled, look for campus events, and spread information across tools that do not know what school or class they belong to.

We started with the schedule because it is the one place where academic life and social life naturally meet.

First product

ClassMate.

The campus app that starts with real course-data scheduling, then organizes reviews and community around it.

Built from campus life

Not another feed. A calmer operating layer.

Students already have enough places to scroll. Our job is to connect the academic source of truth with the honest peer context students need before they choose classes, join communities, or make campus decisions.

Founders

Founded by Kyumin Kwack and Sihyun Park.

the seans began as a student-built product from UC Irvine. Kyumin and Sihyun started with a practical belief: college software should understand a student's actual campus life instead of forcing them to rebuild context across five different tools every quarter.

The company is early by design. We are learning with real students, proving the product at UCI, and expanding campus by campus wherever reliable course data and student verification make ClassMate useful.

Operating principles

Professional software. Student-native instincts.

Context beats volume.

One useful class-specific review is better than another endless feed.

Trust before identity.

Students should be verified by school without being forced to attach their real name to every honest post.

Respect existing tools.

We do not need to replace Discord, Canvas, calendars, Reddit, or group chats. We make campus context easier to find.

Campus by campus.

Expand where the course data, student need, and campus density are real enough for a trusted product.

Roadmap

From one campus to a trusted campus layer.

  1. Now

    Five-school MVP

    Course-data scheduling, saved timetables, reviews, boards, sports events, notifications, and grade tools across UCI, UMD, Cornell, Purdue, and UIUC.

  2. Next

    Real student usage

    More UCI promotion, smoother onboarding beyond TestFlight, stronger review loops, verified anonymous posting, and better campus-specific density.

  3. Later

    More schools

    Expand to additional universities campus by campus when course data and student verification are dependable enough to earn trust.
