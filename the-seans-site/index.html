The product idea

01

Build around real student life.

College is not just registration, grades, or announcements. It is the daily mix of classes, people, questions, decisions, and campus habits that official systems rarely understand.

02

Make peer knowledge trustworthy.

Students already help each other choose classes, understand professors, find housing, join clubs, and survive the week. We want that knowledge to live in verified campus spaces instead of scattered screenshots and rumors.

03

Protect honesty without losing trust.

Real-name social networks make students perform. Open forums are too easy to fake. Our approach is school-verified but personally anonymous, so students can speak honestly inside a community that still has boundaries.

04

Grow campus by campus.

Every university has its own language, data, culture, and routines. We expand where we can build real utility first, then let community grow from trusted academic context.

Company motto

Help students make college make sense.

the seans is building education and community software for the parts of college that happen between official systems: choosing classes, understanding campus life, finding useful peer knowledge, and feeling less alone inside a large university.

Why we started

American campus life has no trusted student layer.

Students piece together university life across calendars, registrar sites, Canvas, Reddit, Discord, RateMyProfessors, group chats, and private notes. Each tool solves a slice of the problem, but none of them are built around the full student experience.

We believe the next student platform in the U.S. will be campus-specific, academically useful, and trusted enough for honest peer discussion.

First product

ClassMate.

Our first product turns the class schedule into a starting point for course decisions, peer knowledge, and campus community.

Currently supported schools: UC Irvine, University of Maryland, Cornell University, Purdue University, and University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign.

Operating principles

Professional software. Student-native instincts.

Context beats volume.

One useful campus-specific answer is better than another endless feed.

Trust before identity.

Students should be verified by school without being forced to attach their real name to every honest post.

Education first.

Community works better when it begins from a real academic need, not from a blank text box asking people to perform.

Campus by campus.

Expand where the course data, student need, and campus density are real enough for a trusted product.

Roadmap

From one useful student tool to a trusted campus layer.

  1. Now

    ClassMate MVP

    A mobile schedule, course review, and campus discussion product that proves students will start from academic utility and return for peer context.

  2. Next

    Campus trust and density

    Smoother onboarding beyond TestFlight, stronger review loops, verified anonymous posting, and enough student activity for each campus to feel alive.

  3. Later

    Education and community platform

    Build the trusted student layer for more universities, expanding campus by campus when data, verification, and real student need are strong enough.
