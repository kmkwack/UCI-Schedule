What we're doing

Help students make college make sense.

Every campus has an unofficial curriculum — the knowledge that actually determines how students experience college — and it has never had a permanent home. The Seans is building it.

Why we started

Enrollment period. Six tabs open. Still not sure you made the right call.

The registrar for availability. RateMyProfessors for a gut-check. Reddit for the real opinions. A spreadsheet to model conflicts. Discord to ask someone who took it last year. Then back to the registrar to actually enroll. Every quarter, same routine — because none of this information exists in one place, or any official place at all.

That's the gap. Not a feature missing from an existing product, but an entire layer of campus life that has never been built. The Seans is building it.

The product idea

01

Your schedule, actually useful.

See today's classes at a glance, check what's coming up, track how far through the quarter you are. The schedule is the starting point — not a separate tool you have to open somewhere else.

02

Course reviews from people at your school.

Ratings from students who had the same professor, in the same department, on the same campus. Not aggregated from everywhere — just from here. Honest because it's anonymous, useful because it's specific.

03

Ask anything without it getting weird.

Your email is verified by the school. Your posts aren't tied to your name. So you can actually ask if a professor is hard to talk to, what a neighborhood is like before signing a lease, or whether anyone else is struggling in a class.

04

Build your schedule before enrollment opens.

Browse real sections, compare options, set up backup plans — all before your enrollment window. When it's time, you know exactly what you're registering for.

How it fits together

Everything connects because it all starts with your actual schedule.

Your classes are the one thing every student has in common. So we build from there. Reviews for the courses you're in. Boards for students on the same campus. Friends from the same sections. It's not a social network layered on top of an academic tool — it's one thing, because those two parts of college were never really separate.

Why us

We came from a place where this already existed.

In Korea, there is a single app that students use to navigate university life — courses, reviews, campus communities, peer knowledge. It is embedded in how students experience college. The gap between what students know and what official systems capture simply does not feel that wide, because the platform closes it.

We came to UC Irvine as international students and noticed the absence immediately. Not as a product opportunity — as a daily friction that made campus feel harder to belong to than it should have been. The Seans is the answer we wished existed when we arrived.

Roadmap

Starting with the stuff students actually need, then building from there.

  1. 01

    Be genuinely useful every single week

    The bar for a new app is high. Students have enough going on. ClassMate has to earn its place on the home screen by solving real problems every term — not just at enrollment, not just at finals.

  2. 02

    Make campus feel smaller

    Find people in your classes. Ask questions without worrying who sees them. Talk about campus stuff with people who are actually there. The community part works because the academic part already does.

  3. 03

    One school at a time, done properly

    Each school has its own course system, its own culture, its own way of doing things. We add schools when we can actually build something that fits — not just flip a switch and call it supported.

  4. 04

    The defining student platform for American higher education

    Every major market has a platform that owns student life at the university level. In the U.S., that position is still open. ClassMate is our first step toward it — and The Seans is the company that intends to fill it.

The long game

Every campus has a student layer that official systems have never been able to capture. We're building it.
